User Experience

UX isn't just about drawing pretty pictures or building beautiful interfaces. That comes later. But first we need to understand what it is the business is trying to accomplish.

UX Cares About Your Business Goals

  1. Are you trying to keep your existing users more engaged?
  2. Are you trying to increase revenue by adding features or building apps that keep the user engaged?
  3. Are you trying to increase productivity or reduce churn?
  4. Are you trying to increase revenue or discover pain points that are costing money and increased customer support?
  5. Are you trying to develop new features or new software to help Marketing achieve their goals?
  6. Are you trying to bring in more users?

UX Helps You Understand Your Users

It's very important to understand how users interact with your system. Your users are the lifeblood of your company. Whether they are your employees or your customers, how they feel affects their engagement, their productivity and their willingness to spend more money with your company. UX aims to understand this by using a variety of techniques:

  1. Sifting through analytics data
  2. Interviewing users ideally in their environment (especially for employees, as understanding where they are using the software can affect what platform and how the interface works)
  3. Observing users in action, either by shadowing them as they use the software, or doing usability testing

UX Helps you strategically allocate resources

When you're a company, your resources are limited. Developing new features or upgrading existing ones And hiring additional resources costs money and time. UX helps to understand where those resources can be best allocating by understanding how users interact with your software or system and how that affects engagement, profits and other factors.

My Approach to UX

The purpose of UX research is to surface pain points and opportunities. It's important to understand first the big picture.

Discover

  1. Work with development, design, marketing to understand the problem.
  2. Ask questions.
  3. Build a bridge with other teams that I work with.

Ideate

  1. Explore various solutions to the problem
  2. Refine the ideas into a set of proposals
  3. Discuss with business
  4. Validate assumptions and ideas

Build

  1. Be open to learning new tools, technologies, processes
  2. Always learn, always advance my skills forward
  3. Try to build consensus with teams.
